About Me

Hello! I'm Kenneth Xu, a junior pursuing a double major in Computer Science and Cognitive Science at the University of Michigan. I'm passionate in bridging technology and human perception—whether I’m building full-stack web apps or exploring AI’s creative side.


I am currently a member of Professor Xu Wang's Lifelong Learning Lab where I'm enhancing collaborative writing with LLMs. I am also conducting research on 3D Segmentation using 3D Gaussian Splats, mentored by Professor Chen Jiasi.


When I'm not in the lab or coding, you can find me playing competitive table tennis, cooking up favorites like omurice and steak, lifting, or playing basketball.

Research Interests

I am deeply fascinated by the interplay between humans and technology, which drives my research in Human Computer Interaction (HCI). My work focuses on designing intuitive, user-centric interfaces and systems that not only enhance the overall user experience but also empower individuals to interact with technology in more natural, seamless ways.


I'm also passionate about the applications of AI x Computer Vision. I investigate how computer vision technologies, when combined with AI, can create dynamic and innovative applications—from augmented reality experiences to assistive technologies. Alongside this technical pursuit, I'm also interested in examining the transparency and ethical dimensions of AI.


I am always eager to connect with fellow researchers, industry professionals, or anyone interested in these topics. Whether you're looking to collaborate on a project, share insights, or simply have a chat about the future of AI and HCI, feel free to reach out!
